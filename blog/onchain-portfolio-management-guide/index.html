Onchain Portfolio Management: Why BSKTs Are the Future

Alvara Protocol · March 5, 2025 · 8 min read

Portfolio management has always been a gated profession. If you wanted to manage money for other people, you needed licenses, legal entities, bank relationships, and enough capital to make the overhead worthwhile. The result: a system where the best investment ideas often come from people who can't actually offer them to the public.

That's changing. Onchain portfolio management strips away the gatekeepers and lets anyone with a sound strategy put it into practice. With Alvara Protocol's BSKTs (basket tokens built on the ERC-7621 standard), portfolio creation takes minutes instead of months, costs nothing instead of thousands, and is open to participants worldwide instead of accredited investors only.

What's Wrong with Traditional Portfolio Management

The traditional fund management industry runs on a model that hasn't fundamentally changed in decades. Here are the core problems:

High barriers to entry

Launching a hedge fund in the US typically requires $50,000 to $100,000 in legal and compliance costs before you manage a single dollar. Mutual funds cost even more. These costs filter out small, innovative managers and concentrate power among established firms.

Opacity by default

Most funds report holdings quarterly, with a 45-day delay. Investors have no real-time visibility into what they own. This creates information asymmetry that benefits managers at the expense of investors. The FTX collapse showed exactly how dangerous this opacity can be, as billions in customer funds were misallocated without anyone outside the company knowing.

Excessive fees

The traditional "2 and 20" fee structure (2% management fee plus 20% of profits) has eroded somewhat, but the average actively managed mutual fund still charges around 0.68% annually. Layer on trading costs, administrative fees, and distribution costs, and the real drag on performance is often well above 1%. Over a 30-year horizon, a 1% fee difference can reduce final portfolio value by 25% or more.

Limited access

The best-performing funds are often closed to new investors or require minimum investments of $250,000 to $1 million. Geographic restrictions add another layer. A skilled analyst in Lagos or Jakarta can't easily invest in a fund domiciled in the Cayman Islands, regardless of their capital or sophistication.

How BSKTs Fix This

BSKTs are onchain basket tokens that represent a portfolio of underlying assets. Think of them as a programmable wrapper around a specific investment strategy. They're built on the ERC-7621 token standard, which defines how basket tokens are created, managed, rebalanced, and dissolved.

Here's why that matters for portfolio management:

Permissionless creation

Anyone can create a BSKT. There's no application process, no minimum AUM, no licensing requirement. If you have a thesis on which tokens will outperform over the next year, you can express that thesis as a BSKT and make it available to others. The total cost is a gas fee, typically under $10 on L2 networks.

Real-time transparency

Every BSKT's holdings are visible onchain at all times. There's no quarterly reporting delay, no window dressing (where managers buy winning stocks right before reporting dates), and no possibility of hidden positions. Investors can verify exactly what they hold, at any moment, without relying on a fund administrator's word.

Programmable fee structures

BSKT managers set their own fees, and these fees are encoded in the smart contract. They can't be changed retroactively or applied selectively. Competition drives fees down naturally because investors can compare fee structures across BSKTs transparently and switch with a single transaction.

Global access, 24/7

BSKTs trade on decentralized exchanges with no market close, no settlement delay, and no geographic restrictions. An investor in Buenos Aires has the same access as one in Boston. Entry and exit happen in seconds, not days.

The BSKT Manager Role

Onchain portfolio management through Alvara isn't just about buying and holding a static basket of tokens. BSKT managers actively manage their portfolios, and the protocol incentivizes good performance.

Building a verifiable track record

In traditional finance, track records are self-reported and often difficult to verify independently. Onchain, your track record is your transaction history. Every rebalance, every asset swap, and every fee collection is recorded permanently. This creates a reputation system based on actual performance, not marketing materials.

Earning management fees

BSKT managers earn fees from investors who hold their basket tokens. The fee is set at creation and collected automatically by the protocol. Good managers attract more capital, which means more fees, creating a direct incentive to perform well. Unlike traditional funds, there's no minimum fund size needed to make management economically viable. A manager with $10,000 in AUM can operate just as efficiently as one with $10 million.

Composability as a competitive advantage

BSKTs are ERC-7621 tokens, a standard built on top of ERC-721 (NFTs), which means each basket is a unique onchain asset that can hold an unlimited number of ERC-20 tokens inside it. They can be used as collateral in lending protocols, traded on DEXs, or even included in other BSKTs. This composability creates opportunities that don't exist in traditional finance. A manager could create a "BSKT of BSKTs," essentially building a fund-of-funds with zero additional overhead.

Why Onchain Beats Off-Chain

Some might argue that centralized platforms can offer similar features without the complexity of blockchain. Here's why onchain portfolio management is structurally better:

  • Self-custody: Investors hold their BSKT tokens in their own wallets. No counterparty risk from exchange failures, bank runs, or corporate fraud. Your assets are yours.
  • Censorship resistance: No single entity can freeze your portfolio, block your redemptions, or prevent you from trading. This matters more than people think until they need it.
  • Atomic transactions: Rebalancing a BSKT happens in a single transaction. There's no settlement risk, no failed trades to reconcile, and no timing gaps between buying and selling.
  • Auditability: Smart contract code is open source and auditable. The rules governing a BSKT aren't buried in a 200-page prospectus. They're visible in the contract, verifiable by anyone with the technical knowledge to read them.
  • Interoperability: BSKTs work with the broader DeFi ecosystem immediately. No API integrations, no partnership agreements, no technical onboarding. Because the underlying assets inside a BSKT are standard ERC-20 tokens, they integrate naturally with existing DeFi infrastructure.

Who Should Be Managing Onchain Portfolios?

The beauty of permissionless systems is that they let the market decide who the best managers are. That said, several groups stand to benefit most from BSKT management:

Crypto-native analysts who have been sharing alpha on Twitter or Discord but have never had a way to monetize their insights directly. Creating a BSKT lets them put real capital behind their calls and earn fees from followers who trust their judgment.

DeFi protocols looking to offer curated exposure to their ecosystem. A lending protocol could create a BSKT of all the major assets it supports, giving users a single token that represents diversified exposure.

DAOs that want to manage their treasuries more actively. Instead of holding a static mix of stablecoins and governance tokens, a DAO could allocate treasury funds to BSKTs managed by proven onchain managers.

Traditional fund managers who want to experiment with onchain strategies without the overhead of launching a new fund vehicle. A BSKT can serve as a proof of concept for a strategy before committing to the full regulatory process.
